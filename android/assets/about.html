From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia:

The Dreamspell is an esoteric calendar based on a modern interpretation of the Maya calendar by New Age spiritualist, Mayanist philosopher, and author José Argüelles, in 1987.

In 2005 (Blue Crystal Storm), I received a copy of a DreamSpell calendar from our local food co-op in Olympia. This began quite an interesting journey as, being a computer programmer with a degree in English literature, I had finally found a "system" that could be used to help understand or explain or better yet to model life experience.

I have always been interested in nature, numbers and colors. I remember my favorite discussion during a high-school English class, a substitute teacher brought a college-level lecture on the usage of color in The Great Gatsby, by F. Scott Fitzgerald. As my brother says, I have a "poetical" relationship with numbers. And while growing up, I wanted to be a naturalist after being immersed and inspired by the wonderful children's books my parents provided and growing up spending so much time outside.

A Note Regarding DreamSpell, Oracles and Divination Systems in General

"The Mayan DreamSpell calendar by Jose Arguelles is based on the Mayan Tzolkin." - Orion kin 66

In the interest of full disclosure and in respect of openness, it is appropriate to provide the following links to quality critiques of the DreamSpell calendar system:

  • THE KEY TO DREAMSPELL Oxib Ajmac / John Major Jenkins
  • Dreamspell correspondence 1
  • Dreamspell correspondence 2

  • Fallacies Of The Dreamspell Calendar

I think it is reasonable and worthwhile to call out this quote from the above "Fallacies" link, which summarizes nicely the issues:

For those who abandoned following Dreamspell, the reasons given are as follows (distilled from hundreds of email and letters since 1992):
  1. Realized that it was not aligned with the Mayan day-count.
  2. Believed they had been misled by the general claim or implication that the Dreamspell system was the same as the Mayan calendar (clear disclosure of the truth was absent).
  3. Detected a cultic displeasure when questions were raised during group meetings.
  4. Personal experiences with the Dreamspell creator and/or inner circle that were.
  5. Felt they had been hypnotized.

I think that the above reasons for abandoning DreamSpell are all potentially legitimate, but also I think this is perhaps good advice for anyone wishing to employ a system of oracular or divinational energies. I think these issues are really related to an individual's ability to discern and prioritize information; i.e. don't believe everything you read on the Internet - same goes for reading fortune cookies, tea leaves, chicken bones and guts, I Ching, pendulums, tarot, astrology or even Wooji boards. The magic of a divination system lies within your special relationship as both participant and observer with the energy of the Universe. Quantum Physics describes the Uncertainty Principle, that is, the inability to simultaneously know the velocity and direction of a particle; the secret to your relationship with an oracle lies within the uncertainty, it is your responsibility and obligation to filter out and discover what resonates with you, regardless of which source the message is coming from. Holding or pinning down to factual accuracies, that is to 'ensure certainty', removes the source of mystery which is the source of the oracle.

While the first point, non-alignment with traditional Mayan day-count, is obviously the largest factual/accuracy hurdle to overcome, I think it can be balanced by correctly framing the intent and purpose of the DreamSpell calendar - which is to say, not as a direct translation or replacement of the Mayan Calendar, for which it is obviously not, but to me, it is a companion learning tool akin to training wheels, to help a single-dimensional Gregorian Calendar mindset to begin to develop the capacity to think in dualistic terms (i.e. solar and lunar, zero and one, etc.), reflecting a more natural order of reality.

DreamSpell makes a compromise with the traditional Mayan day-count to make it more accessible to Western minds, to whom seeing this kind of system integrated within our own daily calender could and can unlock opportunities. I also suspect that DreamSpell, even though while using a perpetual calendar template, will in fact become less necessary or useful as we evolve past the point of needing it, which I think is original intent of Dr. José Argüelles – that is to best prepare us for this time of transition we are all living through.

By saying that because DreamSpell does not meet this unmandated requirement to adhere 100% to the Mayan day-count, that it has no intrinsic value is missing the point and simply throwing the baby out with the bath water. The true strength in DreamSpell, or any other oracle or divination system is the capacity for which it unlocks our stored verbal library of collective historical psychological information. These systems are more interesting in the dynamics they describe than in the concreteness of when and where and what and how, but instead, much more interesting is to focus on why or why not or even seeking inspiration for action or non-action. But ultimately, the responsibility for this relationship rests with the individual, ultimately the Universe wishes you to be happy and will provide and be what ever it is you wish; if you keep a strong sense of self identity and don't get carried away, respect and honor your oracle, you will enjoy a manifold reciprocation!
